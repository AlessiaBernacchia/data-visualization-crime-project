Have you ever though about how the crimes are distributed through the day?

The crime rate trough Boston is variable and changes with the hour of the day. This iformation is very useful to understand when is best to visit the city.

This graph illustrates the average number of crimes in a day over a four-year period in Boston.
The data shows a peak in criminal activity at 17:00, while there is a noticeable drop between 03:00 and 06:00.
Most crimes tend to occur during the day.

Distribution of crimes based on dangerousness and districts.

From this graph we can see how the crimes are distributed in Boston based on their gravity. We can see the districts where crimes are more concentrated. Crimes are subdivided from lower (part 03) to higher (part 01) dangerousness.
This illustration is very useful to understand where would be best to live or visit Boston.

low

UCR Part 03

These crimes include situations such as traffic tickets, traffic infractions. As we can see, sectors B2, B3, D4, C11 have the highest concentration. 

medium

UCR Part 02

These crimes include more dangerous situations such as, serious traffic offenses, accidents, property damage, robberies and others. As we can see, sectors B2, B3, D4, C11 have the highest concentration. 

high

UCR Part 01

These crimes are the most serious, mainly personal injury such as murder, rape, armed robbery. As we can see sectors D4, A1 have the highest concentration. 

Crimes devided by district and by dangerousness.

The low level crimes are of course in higher percentuality over the total crimes. B2, C11 and B4 are the districts with the higest crime rate of Boston, while A7, E5 and A15 are the lowest.

A15

In General from the data before analysed we can see that the safest place to live in Boston is the district A15, also known as Charlestown.
For the tourists visiting Boston is would be recomended to not visit the districts D4, B2, C11, and A1 during the afternoons

We don't have the data to confirm this hypotesis but this district could be associated with an higer overall GDP.
