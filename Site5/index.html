Is Boston a
SAFE PLACE ?

Boston, the bustling capital of Massachusetts,
is home to over 650,000 residents
with an urban density of 1,000 people per square kilometer.
But just how safe is it to live or visit this vibrant city?


This website is dedicated to exploring crime data in Boston,
providing real insights that are valuable
for both residents and visitors.
Browse through our resources for
a comprehensive understanding of the city’s safety.

The city of Boston offers a unique combination of history, culture, and innovation, with distinct neighborhoods that reflect its rich diversity.
From the cobblestone streets of Beacon Hill to the modern skyscrapers of the Financial District, Boston continues to be a dynamic and influential city.


Our research aims to provide an in-depth understanding of safety across the various districts of Boston. To identify the different police districts in the city, we will use the official police designations, where each code corresponds to a specific geographic area within the city.

We will start by analyzing how crimes are distributed throughout the day and then examine the various types of crime, providing a comprehensive overview of crime in Boston. This will allow us to identify the safest districts and those that are best avoided for a solo trip.
All our data will be supported by detailed statistics and graphs, ensuring a clear and accurate representation of the information.

Have you ever though about how the crimes are distributed through the day?

The crime rate in Boston varies significantly
depending on the time of day.
This information is incredibly useful
for understanding the best times to visit the city safely.

This graph illustrates the average number of crimes in a day over a four-year period in Boston.

The peak in criminal activity at 5:00 PM can be attributed to several factors. This time coincides with the end of the workday, when many people are commuting home, running errands, or engaging in social activities. The increased number of people on the streets and in public places provides more opportunities for crimes such as theft, assault, and vandalism.

On the other hand, the significant drop in criminal activity between 3:00 AM and 6:00 AM is likely due to the fact that most people are asleep during these hours.
With fewer people out and about, there are fewer opportunities for crimes to occur. Additionally, the cover of darkness during these early morning hours may deter some criminal activities.


Do all crimes have the same impact?
How are they distributed across Boston?

The police districts in Boston categorize crimes into three levels of severity:
low (UCR Part 03), medium (UCR Part 02), and high (UCR Part 01).
The acronym UCR stands for Uniform Crime Reporting, a system used to classify and monitor crimes. Understanding this categorization can help you navigate the city more safely. The level UCR Part 03 is not an official classification. It has been introduced specifically for this context to decode and represent minor offenses or low-level crimes.

low

UCR Part 03

These are generally considered low-level crimes that do not cause harm to others, including traffic tickets, traffic infractions, loitering, and public intoxication.

The districts most affected by these offenses are Roxbury (B2) and Dorchester (C11), each reporting over 20,000 cases. These infractions, while numerous, typically involve fines and do not pose a significant threat to public safety.

medium

UCR Part 02

These crimes can cause harm to individuals or represent a potential danger to others, they cover fraud, vandalism, simple assault, and robberies.

Notably, these offenses are concentrated in Roxbury (B2), which has reported over 15,000 cases. The impact of these crimes can range from financial loss to physical harm, making them a significant concern for residents and visitors alike.

high

UCR Part 01

These offenses are the most severe and have the greatest impact on victims and the community, involving personal injury such as murder, rape, and armed robbery.

The South End (D4) district has the highest concentration of these serious crimes, with more than 10,000 cases reported. These crimes require substantial law enforcement resources and community support to address and prevent.

Then, in a nutshell?



The data collected over the past four years reveals an interesting picture: low-level crimes (in yellow) predictably make up the majority of the total. However, a closer analysis of Boston's districts reveals a stark contrast between areas with the highest and lowest crime rates. Roxbury (B2), Dorchester (C11), and South End (D4) stand out as the most affected districts, with an alarming mix of serious (in red) and medium-level crimes (in orange). Conversely, East Boston (A7), West Roxbury (E5), and Charlestown (A15) are at the opposite end of the spectrum, showing a more contained level of crime.
These contrasts raise an important question:

what can the safer district teach us to improve the situation in the more critical areas?

Charlestown (A15)

Charlestown, Boston's oldest neighborhood, stands out for its historical charm, cobblestone streets, and a tight-knit community. Once known for its turbulent past, it has undergone a remarkable transformation and is now one of the safest districts, with a crime rate well below the national average.

Charlestown’s safety is bolstered by an active community engaged in local initiatives and the presence of historical landmarks like the Bunker Hill Monument, which draw attention and enhance surveillance in the area.

Additionally, urban redevelopment and infrastructure improvements have made the neighborhood particularly appealing to families and professionals, fostering a stable and welcoming environment. This balance between tradition and modernity makes Charlestown a shining example of urban safety.

In conclusion, the most critical time for safety in the city is around 5 PM, a moment when extra caution is advised.
As for the districts, Roxbury (B2), Dorchester (C11), and South End (D4) stand out as the most problematic, but the case of Charlestown (A15) shows that change is possible. Thanks to an active community, historical landmarks that enhance surveillance, and urban redevelopment efforts, Charlestown has transformed into a model of safety and stability.

Even the other districts, while not prominent in crime data, remain interesting places to visit, each with its unique character and cultural or historical offerings that should not be overlooked.
However, for those seeking a walk in complete peace, Charlestown is undoubtedly the ideal destination—a little haven of safety and charm in the heart of Boston.
